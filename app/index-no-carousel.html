How many earths do we need?

Today humanity uses the equivalent of 1.73 Earths per year.

Global Footprint
The ecological footprint is the only metric that measures how much nature we have and how much we use. The number of earth figure shows if the resource demand of a country is sustainable or not. We have 1 earth and if the key figure is above, we are using more resources, than the earth can regenerate in 1 year. The footprint helps countries to improve sustainability and well-being as well as individuals to understand their impact on the planet. For more details please visit the Footprint Network or see the following Explanation video.

Current
The visualization shows the current footprint in Number of earth for the year 2017. You can choose between three different modes and sizes. With the Plain mode you can see the numbers in different sizes, showing visual the great disbalance among the single countries. The Diff mode emphasize between good and bad in colour and number. The Fill mode finally shows the proportions just by the stroke dense and closeness. The order by Best shows the countries sorted by their footprint value, with the lowest value first.

Over time
Shows the footprint in Number of earth per year over the last decades. This gives a good understanding about the trend, where we heading to. The Plain mode has an additional threshold line indicating if the value is under or over 1. The Diff mode distinguishes between the threshold by colour. The Fill mode shows the differences by colour and area, the more blue the better. To compare the countries among each other, please select the according scale. The order by Best shows the countries sorted by the average value of the time period.
